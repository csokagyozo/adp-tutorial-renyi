# Privacy, confidentiality, and responsible research use

Before uploading material or pasting text into ChatGPT, consider which account or interface you are using. Personal ChatGPT accounts, institutional or business-style accounts, API-style access, and ADP access may have different privacy settings, data handling rules, retention policies, and available controls. For ADP, the relevant rules are the ADP and HUN-REN rules, not simply the rules of a personal subscription.

As a general rule, avoid entering personal data. Formulate questions in general terms whenever possible, without names or identifiable details. Do not upload confidential or restricted material unless you know that you are allowed to do so and, where necessary, the required institutional approvals are in place. This includes referee reports, grant evaluations, private correspondence, personal data, special-category personal data, health or biometric data, data concerning children, student data, employee-performance data, unpublished work by others, confidential institutional documents, and manuscripts or notes shared under an expectation of confidentiality. For joint work, it is good practice to make sure that coauthors are comfortable with the intended use.

ADP/ChatGPT must not be used for decisions or decision preparation concerning real persons. In particular, it should not be used for educational evaluation, admission-related assessment, assessment of a student’s progress, employee-related evaluation, customer-related decisions, automated decision-making, profiling, or other actual applications that affect employees, students, clients, or other real persons.

Authors remain responsible for the final result. ChatGPT should not be treated as an author: it cannot take responsibility for claims, approve the final manuscript, handle conflicts of interest, or sign publication agreements. Substantial use of AI in writing, figure preparation, data analysis, code generation, or research assistance may need to be disclosed, depending on the journal or publisher. Publication rules differ and change over time, so check the current policy of the target journal, conference, or publisher before submission. When in doubt, disclose AI assistance briefly and factually, and make clear that the authors reviewed all output and take responsibility for the final content.

Any incident or suspected incident connected to the platform, AI use, input, output, personal data, confidential data, prohibited use, high-risk AI use, or information security should be reported immediately.
