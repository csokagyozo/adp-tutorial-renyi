# Choosing a good question for the workshop

The workshop will be most useful if you bring a question that is concrete enough to work on, but not so narrow that it becomes only a formatting task. Good examples include: checking a proof attempt for gaps, looking for examples or counterexamples, understanding a definition or section of a paper, improving the exposition of a draft, designing a small computation, or debugging LaTeX or code.

It helps if you bring some context: the definitions, notation, assumptions, a partial attempt, and a clear description of what you want to achieve. Instead of asking “Can ChatGPT solve my problem?”, try to formulate a smaller task: “Can it find a flaw?”, “Can it suggest examples?”, “Can it explain this obstruction?”, or “Can it help me test special cases?”

Avoid questions that are too vague, too large, too confidential, or too dependent on unverified literature. ChatGPT is most useful when the human researcher remains in control of the problem and uses the model for exploration, checking, reformulation, computation, or exposition.

## ideas

 - check a proof attempt for gaps
 - find small examples or counterexamples
 - explain a definition or paper section
 - improve the exposition of a paragraph
 - design a computational experiment
 - debug LaTeX or code
